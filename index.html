Research Brief · Final Month · 마지막 한 달

Build the strategy on evidence, not on instinct.

감으로 쌓지 말고, 근거로 쌓자.

Before designing categories, templates, or pipelines, we need to understand what people actually follow personal accounts for, what builds long-term credibility, and where the underserved space exists for a US-facing wellness and contemplative voice in 2026.

For 이태근 · Theo From Alex Kim · 김승준 Date April 27, 2026 Phase Week 1 of 4
01 · 임무 · Mandate

Why research first.왜 리서치부터 해야 하는가

A content system built on assumed categories will produce assumed engagement. We are not in this for short-term posting volume. We are building the long-term founder authority that will carry every brand AVO launches.

The temptation is to skip research and start designing templates. Don't. The templates are easy. The strategy underneath them is the entire game. If we pick the wrong pillars, the templates render the wrong thing forever.

Your first week is dedicated to one job: understand who actually wins on social as a personal voice in the US wellness, health, and contemplative space, and why. Everything else (templates, pipeline, scheduler, content) is built on top of what you find.

Principle · 원칙

Authentic alignment with Alex is necessary but not sufficient. Categories must also map to what people actually want to follow over years, not what feels right in a brainstorm.

알렉스와의 진정성 있는 정렬은 필수이지만, 그것만으로는 충분하지 않다. 카테고리는 사람들이 수년간 진심으로 팔로우하고 싶어하는 것에 부합해야 한다.
02 · 다섯 가지 질문 · The 5 Research Questions

What we need to know.우리가 알아야 할 다섯 가지

Five questions structure the research. Each one has sub-questions. By end of Week 1, you should have a 1-2 page write-up answering each, with sources cited.

Question 01

Why do people follow personal accounts in 2026, and what makes them stay?

2026년에 사람들은 왜 개인 계정을 팔로우하며, 무엇이 그들을 머물게 하는가?

The psychology question. Without this, every other choice is guesswork.

  • What emotional needs does following a personal account meet? (Validation, learning, community, escapism, aspiration, parasocial intimacy?)
  • What's the difference between a follow-and-stay account vs a follow-and-unfollow-in-a-week account?
  • How does parasocial relationship form, and what breaks it?
  • What signals trust in 2026? (Receipts, consistency, vulnerability, expertise, lifestyle, proximity?)
  • What signals fakeness? (And how does the audience detect it?)
Question 02

What categories of content drive long-term retention vs short-term engagement spikes?

장기 유지율을 만드는 콘텐츠 카테고리 vs 단기 참여 폭발을 만드는 카테고리는?

A viral post and a loyal follower are not the same thing. We want followers who stay for years.

  • Which content categories have shortest half-life? (Trends, hot takes, quote cards, news reactions)
  • Which categories compound? (Education, frameworks, recurring rituals, longitudinal stories)
  • What's the optimal ratio of evergreen vs timely content for personal authority accounts?
  • Are there content categories that drive engagement but actively harm credibility long-term?
Question 03

What does credibility look like in the US wellness and contemplative space specifically?

미국 웰니스 및 명상/영성 영역에서 신뢰는 구체적으로 어떻게 만들어지는가?

Credibility in this space is contested. The wellness industry has a credibility crisis, which is also our opportunity.

  • Who are the most-trusted voices in US wellness right now, and how did they earn that trust?
  • What are the credibility-destroying patterns in this space? (Selling courses, MLM ties, undisclosed ads, supplements with no evidence)
  • How do voices with religious or contemplative grounding land in 2026 US culture vs five years ago?
  • Where does the line sit between credible-curious and crank? Between earnestness and corny?
  • How important is formal credentialing (MD, RD, PhD) vs lived practice authority?
Question 04

What carousel structure and visual patterns work best for personal voice in this space?

개인 보이스를 살리는 카루셀 구조와 시각 패턴은 무엇인가?

The form question. We are automating CAROUSEL specifically. This question affects every template you build.

  • Cover slide patterns that earn the swipe vs ones people scroll past
  • Optimal slide count for retention (3, 5, 7, 10?) and how it varies by content category
  • Image-to-text balance per slide (text-heavy vs photo-heavy vs hybrid)
  • Caption length patterns paired with carousels (short hook vs long-form essay caption)
  • End-card patterns: CTA, soft close, pattern-break, repeat the question
  • Hook patterns specific to personal accounts (vs influencer / brand accounts)
  • How important is face/personality showing in carousels vs all-text or all-photo
Question 05

Where is the underserved space Alex can occupy in US wellness culture?

알렉스가 미국 웰니스 문화에서 차지할 수 있는 비어 있는 자리는?

The white-space question. We don't want to be voice #847 in a saturated lane.

  • What's overproduced in US wellness right now? (Saturation analysis)
  • What's underproduced? (Gap analysis)
  • What does Korean and East Asian wellness tradition uniquely offer that US audiences want but don't have a clear voice for?
  • What does a Christian-curious or contemplative voice offer in a wellness space that has been heavily New Age / mystical?
  • What does a CPG-builder perspective offer that pure wellness-influencer perspective doesn't?
  • Which niches inside wellness are growing fastest in 2026? (Microbiome, longevity, regenerative ag, raw milk, slow living, men's health, etc.)
03 · 출처 · Sources & Methods

Where to look. How to look.어디서 찾고, 어떻게 볼 것인가

Primary research methods · 1차 리서치 방법

Method 01 · 계정 감사

Account audit

Pick 15-25 personal accounts in the wellness, health, food, contemplative space. For each: count their posts in last 90 days, categorize by content type, log engagement rate, log growth trajectory if available (Social Blade, ViewStats).

Output: spreadsheet with category x engagement matrix.

Method 02 · 댓글 발굴

Comment archaeology

For top 5 reference accounts, read the comments on their highest-engagement carousels of the last 6 months. What are people actually saying? What needs are being met? What language are they using?

Output: 1-page synthesis of audience emotional language patterns.

Method 03 · 포화도 지도

Saturation map

For each candidate category (food, verse, news, education, practice), sample 30+ accounts and assess saturation. Where is the same content being made by 50 people? Where is it being made by 5?

Output: saturation heatmap by category.

Method 04 · 장문 종합

Long-form synthesis

Read 3-5 long-form pieces (Substack essays, podcast transcripts, books) on personal brand psychology, parasocial dynamics, or wellness culture. Distill principles, not tactics.

Output: principles list with source citations.

Secondary sources to consult · 참고 자료

  • Books: Show Your Work (Austin Kleon), Trust Me, I'm Lying (Ryan Holiday), The Wellness Trap (Christy Harrison), Strangers Drowning (Larissa MacFarquhar) for moral-life voices, anything Wendell Berry on attention.
  • Substacks / newsletters: Casey Means' newsletter, Maintenance Phase (podcast), Plain English (Derek Thompson), The Honest Broker (Ted Gioia) for cultural pattern reading.
  • Studies and reports: Edelman Trust Barometer (annual), Pew Research on US religion and wellness, Reports from Future Market Insights and Mintel on wellness consumer trends 2024-2026.
  • Platforms: Native IG and TikTok analytics on saved 2026 industry case studies. Look at Later, Sprout Social, and Hootsuite annual reports.
  • Industry voices: Casey Lewis (After School newsletter), Jasmine Sun, Anu Atluru on building authority in public.
Caution · 주의

Marketing-blog content on "how to grow on Instagram" is largely garbage. Almost all of it is written for SEO and recycles the same generic advice. Bias your reading toward primary observation (real account audits, real comment data) and toward thinkers who have built audiences themselves.

"인스타그램 성장법" 같은 마케팅 블로그 글은 거의 다 SEO용 재탕이다. 1차 관찰 (실제 계정 감사, 실제 댓글 데이터)과 본인이 청중을 직접 키워본 사람들의 글을 우선시해라.
04 · 카루셀 우선 연구 · Carousel-First Study Methodology

Study the carousel, not the account.계정이 아니라 카루셀을 분석한다

Many reference accounts post Reels, Stories, single images, and carousels. Our automation system is for carousels specifically. So your study must be filtered through a carousel lens. Even when an account uses Reels heavily, your job is to extract their carousel patterns.

What to extract from every carousel you study

Element What to log Why it matters
Cover slide
표지 슬라이드		 Hook type (question, statement, statistic, image-only, name-of-thing). Text size relative to canvas. Use of face vs object vs typography. The cover decides if anyone swipes. Without the swipe, slides 2-10 don't exist.
Slide count
슬라이드 수		 Total slide count per post. Average across the account. Variance by content type. Different counts serve different content. 3-slide rhythm vs 7-slide essay vs 10-slide deep dive.
Slide-to-slide rhythm
슬라이드 간 리듬		 Are slides repetitive in structure or each unique? Does the layout pulse (text → image → text → image)? Rhythm controls swipe-completion rate. A reader either commits at slide 2 or drops out.
Text-image ratio
텍스트와 이미지 비중		 Per slide: text-dominant, image-dominant, or balanced? Average across the account. Tells us what the audience reads vs scans. Affects template design.
Typography hierarchy
서체 위계		 How many type sizes per slide? Is there a clear primary, secondary, tertiary? Serif vs sans? Visual hierarchy is what makes a carousel readable while scrolling fast.
End slide pattern
마지막 슬라이드		 CTA? Soft close? Question? Repeat the cover? Brand mark only? Save reminder? End slides drive saves, shares, follows. The pattern matters more than people realize.
Caption pairing
캡션 짝짓기		 Caption length. Does it expand the carousel content or repeat it? Hook in the first line? Caption is where the algorithm reads context. Affects discovery.
Engagement signal
참여 신호		 Compare carousel posts to Reels posts on the same account. Which gets more comments? Which gets more saves? Saves matter most for retention-focused accounts. Carousels generally win on saves.
Reels vs Carousel · 릴스와 카루셀

If a reference account leans heavily on Reels, you can still extract carousel patterns from their less-frequent carousel posts. Don't skip an account because they're "Reels-first." Find their last 10-15 carousels and study those specifically.

참고 계정이 릴스 위주여도, 그들이 올린 카루셀 게시물 10-15개를 따로 모아서 분석해라. 카루셀 패턴은 카루셀에서만 추출된다.

Output of carousel study (Week 1)

  • Spreadsheet of 30-50 specific carousel posts you studied (link, account, slide count, hook type, end pattern, observed engagement)
  • 1-page synthesis: 5-7 carousel patterns that consistently work, with examples
  • 1-page synthesis: 3-5 carousel patterns to avoid, with examples of why
  • Recommended cover slide vocabulary (hook types) to build into AVO templates
  • Recommended end slide vocabulary (close types) to build into AVO templates

09 · 종합 · Synthesis Framework

How to convert research into recommendations.리서치를 추천안으로 전환하는 법

By Friday of Week 1, you should have findings. By Monday of Week 2, you should have a synthesis that translates findings into actionable recommendations.

The synthesis output should answer six questions

  1. Position · 포지션: Where in the US wellness / contemplative space does Alex sit, given the saturation map and his actual life?
  2. Pillars · 기둥: What 3 to 5 content pillars best serve that position, with evidence from research that each pillar has audience demand and is not fully saturated?
  3. Voice · 보이스: What 5 to 7 voice principles (do's and don'ts) emerged from study of reference and anti-reference accounts?
  4. Carousel format · 카루셀 형식: What carousel structure (cover slide patterns, body slide rhythm, slide count, end card) consistently outperforms in this space?
  5. Cadence · 빈도: What posting frequency produces sustained growth without burnout, based on observed patterns of long-term reference accounts?
  6. Differentiation · 차별점: What 2 to 3 things will be uniquely AVO that nothing else in the space currently does?

Decision framework when research findings conflict · 발견이 충돌할 때 결정 기준

You will encounter conflicts. One reference account does X, another does the opposite. Use this hierarchy:

  1. Long-term retention > short-term engagement. If a tactic drives a viral spike but accounts that use it churn audience faster, reject it.
    장기 유지율 > 단기 참여 폭발
  2. Credibility-building > growth-hacking. If a tactic grows numbers but erodes trust, reject it.
    신뢰 구축 > 그로스 해킹
  3. Sustainable for Alex > theoretically optimal. If a recommendation requires Alex to be someone he isn't (extroverted, religious-traditionalist, science-PhD), reject it. The system has to be runnable.
    알렉스가 지속 가능한 것 > 이론적 최적
  4. Underserved > proven. If two paths both work, the less crowded one builds more durable distinct authority.
    비어 있는 자리 > 검증된 자리
